The fourteenth letter

If I were all alone but were to have many imaginary friends, and if I had not realize that they were illusions; would I still be alone?

What does it mean to not be alone, what can be considered as company? Let us first try to discover an answer to this preliminary question.

Humans, without uncertainty, are a company. Likely also domestic animals of higher intellect like dogs, cats, and parrots. It is doubtful that we can add robots even if they can perform richer interactions than the former. It is generally hard to call anything nonbiological as a companion. For things biological, we must discard those with lesser intellect because there is very little mutual interaction possible with them. Even if you can prune the tree, the tree cannot prune you. This is similarly true for fungi, bacteria, and similar microscopical life forms. Another reason we must exclude simple life forms like bacteria is because we can never be rid of them and therefore if they are not left out the word 'alone' would lose its practical meaning.

Let us sum our findings: A company is a biological intellect that can be parted with. We cannot be parted from ourselves so we cannot be a company to ourselves, so far so good. But possibly multiple personalities can reside in the same body, and possibly they can be parted from each other and be united again.

Therefore the answer is 'maybe not(?)'
